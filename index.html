Know your form

Which ITR form is right for you?

We automatically detect and fill the correct form based on your income profile.

ITR-1
Salaried individuals (Sahaj)
  • Income from salary or pension
  • One house property
  • Other sources (interest, etc.)
  • Total income up to ₹50 lakh
ITR-2
Individuals with capital gains
  • Salary + capital gains (stocks, MF)
  • More than one house property
  • Foreign income or assets
  • No business or profession income
ITR-3
Business owners & professionals
  • Income from business or profession
  • Partner in a firm
  • Freelancers with detailed accounts
  • Any combination of the above
ITR-4
Presumptive income (Sugam)
  • Business under Section 44AD
  • Professionals under 44ADA
  • Transport operators 44AE
  • Income up to ₹50 lakh (profession)
ITR-5
Firms, LLPs & AOPs
  • Partnership firms
  • Limited liability partnerships
  • Association of persons
  • Body of individuals

The due date for filing ITR for FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27) is 31st July 2026 for individuals not subject to audit. Filing before the deadline avoids a late fee of ₹5,000 (₹1,000 if income is below ₹5 lakh).
The new regime offers lower slab rates but removes most deductions (80C, HRA, etc.). The old regime benefits those with high deductions like home loans, large 80C investments, and HRA. Use our calculator to compare both and pick the one with lower tax liability.
If your gross income (before deductions) exceeds ₹2.5 lakh (₹3 lakh for senior citizens), filing is mandatory. Even if tax is zero due to rebate u/s 87A, filing is required if income crosses the basic exemption limit. Filing also helps with visa applications, loan approvals, and carrying forward losses.
You'll need: Form 16 (from employer), bank account statements, AIS/TIS from the IT portal, investment proofs (80C, 80D), home loan interest certificate, and your PAN and Aadhaar. Freelancers also need their invoices and expense records.
For most salaried individuals (ITR-1 and ITR-2), you can absolutely file yourself using FileThisITR — no CA needed. A CA is advisable only for complex cases involving business audits, foreign income, or large capital gains from multiple sources.
E-verification is the final step and must be done within 30 days of filing. The easiest method is via Aadhaar OTP on the Income Tax portal. Alternatives include net banking, Demat account EVC, or sending a signed ITR-V by post to CPC Bengaluru.
